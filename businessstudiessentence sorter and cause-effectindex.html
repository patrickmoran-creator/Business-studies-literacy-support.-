Teacher notes

These games are designed to align with What Works Best and Universal Design for Learning (UDL) principles.

Learning intentions

  • Students recognise and sort simple, compound and complex sentences in Business contexts.
  • Students select appropriate cause–effect connectives and clauses to explain operations and marketing decisions.
  • Students build confidence controlling sentence structure in exam-style writing.

UDL connections

  • Multiple means of engagement: interactive dragging, immediate colour feedback, low-stakes practice.
  • Multiple means of representation: visual sentence sorting, explicit connective and clause choices, teacher-led modelling.
  • Multiple means of action & expression: students can discuss choices orally, type improved sentences, or use the patterns in written exam responses.

Suggestions for use

  • Use Sentence Sort as a warm-up before extended response practice.
  • After the Connectives game, ask students to write one new paragraph using at least three different connectives and clauses.
  • Project the games and “think aloud” as you make decisions about sentence type and cause–effect links.
